19 The concept of 'Prior Art' in patent law refers to:

B. Artistic works created by the inventor previously

34 What defines a Generic Mark, and is it protectable?

A. A suggestive name; protectable
B. A made-up word; protectable
C. A unique name; highly protectable
D. A common name for the product itself (e.g., 'Apple' for apples); not protectable

Correct Answer: A common name for the product itself (e.g., 'Apple' for apples); not protectable

Explanation:

Generic terms describe the category of the product (e.g., 'Computer' for a laptop). They cannot be trademarked because no single company should have a monopoly on the common name of a product.

40 If a patent claim reads: 'A method for curing rubber using degrees of heat,' and a competitor uses degrees purely to evade the literal wording, a court might find infringement under:
