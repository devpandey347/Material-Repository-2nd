1 Which characteristic primarily distinguishes a short story from a novel?

A. It focuses on a single incident or effect.
B. It is always written in the first person.
C. It has no climax.
D. It contains no dialogue.

Correct Answer: It focuses on a single incident or effect.

Explanation:

Short stories are defined by their brevity and focus on a single unity of effect, whereas novels explore multiple subplots and a broader scope.

Incorrect! Try again.

2 According to Edgar Allan Poe, a short story should be readable in which timeframe?

A. Three distinct sessions
B. Over the course of a week
C. A minimum of five hours
D. One single sitting

Correct Answer: One single sitting

Explanation:

Poe argued that to maintain the 'unity of effect,' a short story must be short enough to be read in one sitting, usually ranging from a half-hour to two hours.

Incorrect! Try again.

3 What is the literary term for a sudden realization or flash of insight experienced by a character?

A. Epiphany
B. Flashback
C. Exposition
D. Denouement

Correct Answer: Epiphany

Explanation:

Popularized by James Joyce, an epiphany is a moment where a character achieves a sudden spiritual or intellectual realization.

Incorrect! Try again.

4 Which of the following best defines 'fiction'?

A. Biographical essays
B. Literature created from the imagination
C. A journalistic account of events
D. Writing based strictly on historical facts

Correct Answer: Literature created from the imagination

Explanation:

Fiction refers to literature in the form of prose, especially short stories and novels, that describes imaginary events and people.

Incorrect! Try again.

5 What is 'verisimilitude' in the context of fiction?

A. The appearance of being true or real
B. The use of very short sentences
C. The ending of a story
D. The antagonist's motivation

Correct Answer: The appearance of being true or real

Explanation:

Verisimilitude is the quality in a story that makes it seem realistic and credible to the reader, even if the events are made up.

Incorrect! Try again.

6 Which term describes the author's choice of words?

A. Syntax
B. Theme
C. Plot
D. Diction

Correct Answer: Diction

Explanation:

Diction refers specifically to the linguistic choices (vocabulary and phrasing) a writer makes to convey an idea or establish a voice.

Incorrect! Try again.

7 In a short story, what does 'syntax' refer to?

A. The background setting
B. The emotional atmosphere
C. The arrangement of words and phrases to create sentences
D. The definitions of difficult words

Correct Answer: The arrangement of words and phrases to create sentences

Explanation:

While diction is word choice, syntax is the grammatical structure and order of those words within a sentence.

8 What is the definition of 'non-fiction'?

B. Stories set in the future
C. Poetry that tells a story
D. Prose writing that is based on facts, real events, and real people

Correct Answer: Prose writing that is based on facts, real events, and real people

Explanation:

Non-fiction is distinct from fiction because it claims to portray reality, facts, and actual events rather than imaginary ones.

A. Science Fiction
B. High Fantasy

D. Creative Non-fiction

Correct Answer: Creative Non-fiction

Explanation:

Creative non-fiction (or narrative non-fiction) uses literary styles and techniques to create factually accurate narratives.

10 What is the 'tone' of a story?

A. The feelings the reader has while reading
B. The physical location of the plot
C. The speed at which the story moves
D. The author's attitude toward the subject or audience

Correct Answer: The author's attitude toward the subject or audience

11 How does 'mood' differ from 'tone'?

Correct Answer: Mood is the reader's emotional response; tone is the author's attitude.

Explanation:

Mood refers to the atmosphere or emotional setting created by a piece of literary work, which dictates how the reader feels.

12 A concrete object that represents an abstract idea is called a:

A. Simile
B. Symbol
C. Plot twist
D. Climax

Correct Answer: Symbol

Explanation:

A symbol is a person, object, action, or place that has a literal meaning in the story but suggests or represents other meanings (often abstract).

Correct Answer: The smell of burnt toast hung heavy in the stagnant air.
