1 Which scientist is credited with proposing the theory of Continental Drift, the precursor to Plate Tectonics?

A. Arthur Holmes
B. Harry Hess
C. J. Tuzo Wilson
D. Alfred Wegener

Correct Answer: Alfred Wegener

Explanation:

Alfred Wegener proposed the theory of Continental Drift in 1912, suggesting that continents were once joined in a supercontinent called Pangaea.

Incorrect! Try again.

2 The semi-molten layer of the mantle on which tectonic plates move is known as the:

A. Asthenosphere
B. Mesosphere
C. Lithosphere
D. Inner Core

Correct Answer: Asthenosphere

Explanation:

The asthenosphere is the highly viscous, mechanically weak, and geologically ductile region of the upper mantle below the lithosphere.

Incorrect! Try again.

3 Which type of plate boundary is characterized by plates sliding past each other horizontally?

A. Transform boundary
B. Convergent boundary
C. Subduction zone
D. Divergent boundary

Correct Answer: Transform boundary

Explanation:

At a transform boundary, tectonic plates slide past one another laterally. Crust is neither created nor destroyed here.

Incorrect! Try again.

4 The San Andreas Fault in California is an example of which type of plate margin?

A. Constructive margin
B. Collision margin
C. Destructive margin
D. Conservative margin

Correct Answer: Conservative margin

Explanation:

The San Andreas Fault is a conservative (transform) plate boundary between the Pacific Plate and the North American Plate.

Incorrect! Try again.

5 The formation of the Himalayas is primarily attributed to the collision between which two tectonic plates?

A. Indian and Eurasian
B. Nazca and South American
C. African and Arabian
D. Pacific and North American

Correct Answer: Indian and Eurasian

Explanation:

The Himalayas were formed (and are still rising) due to the continental-continental collision between the Indian Plate and the Eurasian Plate.

Incorrect! Try again.

6 What is the primary evidence used to support the theory of Sea-Floor Spreading?

A. Fossil distribution
B. Paleomagnetism (Magnetic striping)
C. Coastline fit
D. Glacial striations

Correct Answer: Paleomagnetism (Magnetic striping)

Explanation:

Symmetrical patterns of magnetic reversals (magnetic striping) on either side of mid-ocean ridges provide strong evidence for sea-floor spreading.

Incorrect! Try again.

7 A 'Benioff zone' is associated with which geological feature?

A. Mid-ocean ridges
B. Subduction zones
C. Rift valleys
D. Shield volcanoes

Correct Answer: Subduction zones

Explanation:

A Benioff zone is a planar zone of seismicity corresponding with the down-going slab in a subduction zone.

Incorrect! Try again.

8 The concept of 'Isostasy' refers to:

A. The flow of lava
B. The gravitational equilibrium of the Earth's crust
C. The cycle of erosion
D. The magnetic polarity of rocks

Correct Answer: The gravitational equilibrium of the Earth's crust

Explanation:

Isostasy describes the state of gravitational equilibrium between Earth's crust and mantle such that the crust 'floats' at an elevation that depends on its thickness and density.

Incorrect! Try again.

9 According to the Geosynclinal Theory, the long, narrow, shallow water depressions where sediments accumulate are called:

A. Horsts
B. Cratons
C. Geosynclines
D. Grabens

Correct Answer: Geosynclines

Explanation:

Geosynclines are large-scale depressions in the earth's crust containing very thick deposits of sedimentary rocks, historically believed to be the precursors to fold mountains.

Incorrect! Try again.

10 Which of the following mountain systems was formed during the Alpine Orogeny?

A. The Urals
B. The Appalachians
C. The Caledonian mountains
D. The Alps

Correct Answer: The Alps

Explanation:

The Alpine Orogeny is a mountain-building phase that occurred in the Late Mesozoic and Cenozoic eras, forming the Alps, Himalayas, and Andes.

Incorrect! Try again.

11 In the context of mountain building, what is a 'Nappe'?

A. A type of volcanic intrusion
B. A large-scale recumbent fold that has been thrust forward
C. A vertical fault
D. A basin formation

Correct Answer: A large-scale recumbent fold that has been thrust forward

Explanation:

A nappe is a sheet of rock that has moved sideways over neighboring strata as a result of an overthrust or recumbent fold.

Incorrect! Try again.

12 Block mountains, such as the Vosges in France, are primarily formed by:

A. Folding
B. Erosion
C. Faulting
D. Volcanism

Correct Answer: Faulting

Explanation:

Block mountains (Horsts) are formed when large areas of bedrock are widely broken up by faults creating vertical displacements.

Incorrect! Try again.

13 Kober’s theory of mountain building focuses on the contraction of:

A. The entire globe
B. Continental shields
C. Geosynclines
D. The ocean floor

Correct Answer: Geosynclines

Explanation:

Leopold Kober's theory involves the squeezing of geosynclinal sediments between rigid landmasses he called 'Kratogens'.

Incorrect! Try again.

14 Which term describes a large-scale depression formed by the collapse of a volcano's magma chamber?

A. Vent
B. Fissure
C. Caldera
D. Crater

Correct Answer: Caldera

Explanation:

A caldera is a large cauldron-like hollow that forms shortly after the emptying of a magma chamber in a volcanic eruption, causing the overlying support to collapse.

Incorrect! Try again.

15 Lava with a high silica content causes explosive eruptions because it is:

A. Very hot
B. Low viscosity
C. Rich in iron
D. High viscosity

Correct Answer: High viscosity

Explanation:

High silica content increases the viscosity (thickness) of magma, trapping gases and leading to pressure build-up and explosive eruptions.

Incorrect! Try again.

16 Which intrusive volcanic landform is a horizontal sheet of solidified magma usually intruded between sedimentary layers?

A. Dyke
B. Batholith
C. Sill
D. Laccolith

Correct Answer: Sill

Explanation:

A sill is a tabular sheet intrusion that has intruded between older layers of sedimentary rock, beds of volcanic lava, or tuff.

Incorrect! Try again.

17 A massive, deep-seated intrusion of igneous rock, often forming the core of mountain ranges, is called a:

A. Neck
B. Lopolith
C. Phacolith
D. Batholith

Correct Answer: Batholith

Explanation:

Batholiths are large emplacements of igneous intrusive rock (plutons) that form from cooled magma deep in the Earth's crust.

Incorrect! Try again.

18 The Hawaiian Islands are an example of what type of volcanism?

A. Subduction zone volcanism
B. Hotspot volcanism
C. Mid-ocean ridge volcanism
D. Continental rift volcanism

Correct Answer: Hotspot volcanism

Explanation:

Hawaii is formed by a mantle plume or 'hotspot' located beneath the moving Pacific Plate.

Incorrect! Try again.

19 What is a 'Pyroclastic Flow'?

A. A slow-moving stream of basaltic lava
B. A mudflow caused by volcanic melting of snow
C. A flow of groundwater heated by magma
D. A fast-moving current of hot gas and volcanic matter

Correct Answer: A fast-moving current of hot gas and volcanic matter

Explanation:

Pyroclastic flows are fluidized mixtures of hot rock fragments, hot gases, and entrapped air that move at high speeds.

Incorrect! Try again.

20 Which scale is used to measure the intensity of an earthquake based on observed effects and damage?

A. Moment Magnitude Scale
B. Mohs Scale
C. Mercalli Scale
D. Richter Scale

Correct Answer: Mercalli Scale

Explanation:

The Modified Mercalli Intensity Scale measures the intensity of shaking produced by an earthquake based on people's reports and observed damage.

Incorrect! Try again.

21 The point within the Earth where an earthquake rupture starts is called the:

A. Shadow zone
B. Epicenter
C. Focus (Hypocenter)
D. Fault scarp

Correct Answer: Focus (Hypocenter)

Explanation:

The focus (or hypocenter) is the point of origin of an earthquake underground. The epicenter is the point directly above it on the surface.

Incorrect! Try again.

22 Which seismic waves are the fastest and the first to arrive at a seismic station?

A. P-waves
B. Rayleigh waves
C. S-waves
D. L-waves

Correct Answer: P-waves

Explanation:

Primary waves (P-waves) are compressional waves and travel faster than other seismic waves.

Incorrect! Try again.

23 S-waves (Secondary waves) cannot travel through:

A. The mantle
B. Liquids
C. Solids
D. Gases

Correct Answer: Liquids

Explanation:

S-waves are shear waves and require a medium with shear strength to propagate; therefore, they cannot pass through liquids (like the outer core).

Incorrect! Try again.

24 The 'Elastic Rebound Theory' explains the mechanism of:

A. Mountain folding
B. Volcanic eruptions
C. Tsunami propagation
D. Earthquake generation

Correct Answer: Earthquake generation

Explanation:

Proposed by H.F. Reid, this theory explains how energy is stored in rocks as elastic strain and released suddenly during an earthquake.

Incorrect! Try again.

25 Tsunamis are most commonly generated by:

A. Tides
B. Surface winds
C. Hurricanes
D. Vertical displacement of the ocean floor

Correct Answer: Vertical displacement of the ocean floor

Explanation:

Tsunamis are usually caused by underwater earthquakes at subduction zones that vertically displace a large volume of water.

Incorrect! Try again.

26 In W.M. Davis's Cycle of Erosion, the three main factors controlling landscape development are:

A. Climate, Vegetation, Time
B. Rock type, Slope, Rainfall
C. Uplift, Erosion, Deposition
D. Structure, Process, Stage

Correct Answer: Structure, Process, Stage

Explanation:

Davis famously stated that landscape is a function of structure (geology), process (agents of erosion), and stage (time/age of the cycle).

Incorrect! Try again.

27 The end product of the Davisian cycle of erosion is a low-lying surface called a:

A. Etchplain
B. Peneplain
C. Pediplain
D. Panplain

Correct Answer: Peneplain

Explanation:

A peneplain is a low-relief plain formed by protracted erosion, representing the 'old age' stage of the Davisian cycle.

Incorrect! Try again.

28 Which geomorphologist proposed the concept of 'Slope Replacement' and criticized Davis's time-dependent model?

A. J.T. Hack
B. Walther Penck
C. L.C. King
D. G.K. Gilbert

Correct Answer: Walther Penck

Explanation:

Penck proposed that slopes retreat by replacing flatter lower slopes with steeper upper ones, a concept associated with his 'Morphological Analysis'.

Incorrect! Try again.

29 According to L.C. King, the dominant process in landscape evolution in semi-arid and arid regions is:

A. Solution
B. Down-wearing
C. Glacial scouring
D. Scarp retreat (Back-wearing)

Correct Answer: Scarp retreat (Back-wearing)

Explanation:

L.C. King emphasized 'Pediplanation', where scarp retreat creates broad, flat surfaces called pediments.

Incorrect! Try again.

30 A 'Monadnock' refers to:

A. A river terrace
B. A volcanic vent
C. An isolated hill of resistant rock on a peneplain
D. A type of glacial deposit

Correct Answer: An isolated hill of resistant rock on a peneplain

Explanation:

In the Davisian system, a monadnock is an isolated residual hill standing above a peneplain because the rock is more resistant to erosion.

Incorrect! Try again.

31 The concept of 'Dynamic Equilibrium' in landscape development was proposed by:

A. W.M. Davis
B. J.T. Hack
C. Charles Lyell
D. Alfred Wegener

Correct Answer: J.T. Hack

Explanation:

J.T. Hack argued that landscapes adjust to reach a state of equilibrium where erosion balances uplift, independent of evolutionary 'stages'.

Incorrect! Try again.

32 What landform is formed by the coalescence of pediments according to L.C. King?

A. Peneplain
B. Pediplain
C. Floodplain
D. Delta

Correct Answer: Pediplain

Explanation:

King theorized that as scarps retreat, the pediments at their bases merge to form an extensive flat surface known as a Pediplain.

Incorrect! Try again.

33 The term 'Primarumpf', referring to an initial convex uplifted surface, is associated with:

A. Penck
B. Davis
C. Hutton
D. King

Correct Answer: Penck

Explanation:

Walther Penck used the term 'Primarumpf' to describe a primary peneplain or initial surface that is uplifted without significant relief initially.

Incorrect! Try again.

34 Rejuvenation of a river cycle is often indicated by the presence of:

A. Knickpoints and river terraces
B. Ox-bow lakes
C. Meanders
D. Deltas

Correct Answer: Knickpoints and river terraces

Explanation:

Rejuvenation (renewed downcutting due to uplift or sea-level drop) creates breaks in the slope (knickpoints) and stranded floodplains (terraces).

Incorrect! Try again.

35 Which theory suggests that landscapes are formed by the stripping of deeply weathered saprolite?

A. Etchplanation
B. Glaciation
C. Pediplanation
D. Peneplanation

Correct Answer: Etchplanation

Explanation:

Etchplanation involves the chemical weathering of bedrock underground and the subsequent stripping of the weathered material (saprolite) to expose the 'etch surface'.

Incorrect! Try again.

36 The 'Base Level of Erosion' concept was first introduced by:

A. J.W. Powell
B. W.M. Davis
C. G.K. Gilbert
D. A. Penck

Correct Answer: J.W. Powell

Explanation:

J.W. Powell introduced the concept of base level, the lowest point to which a river can erode its channel (usually sea level).

Incorrect! Try again.

37 An 'Inselberg' is most closely associated with which landscape cycle?

A. Arid/Semi-arid cycle (Pediplanation)
B. Karst cycle
C. Glacial cycle
D. Coastal cycle

Correct Answer: Arid/Semi-arid cycle (Pediplanation)

Explanation:

Inselbergs are isolated rock hills or knobs that rise abruptly from a gently sloping or virtually level surrounding plain, typical in King's pediplanation cycle.

Incorrect! Try again.

38 Polycyclic or Multi-cyclic landscapes result from:

A. Multiple interruptions by uplift or climate change
B. A single uninterrupted period of erosion
C. Volcanic deposition only
D. Wind erosion

Correct Answer: Multiple interruptions by uplift or climate change

Explanation:

Polycyclic landscapes show evidence of multiple cycles of erosion, often due to tectonic uplift or sea-level changes interrupting the previous cycle.

Incorrect! Try again.

39 The term 'Panplain', suggesting a surface formed by lateral planation of rivers, was proposed by:

A. Davis
B. Strahler
C. Penck
D. Crickmay

Correct Answer: Crickmay

Explanation:

C.H. Crickmay critiqued Davis and proposed that lateral erosion (planation) by migrating rivers is the primary agent in flattening landscapes, forming a Panplain.

Incorrect! Try again.

40 Which drainage pattern is likely to form on a dome or volcanic cone?

A. Dendritic
B. Radial
C. Rectangular
D. Trellis

Correct Answer: Radial

Explanation:

Radial drainage occurs when streams flow outward from a central high point, like a volcano or dome.

Incorrect! Try again.

41 A 'Graben' is a feature associated with:

A. Glacial erosion
B. Shear forces
C. Tensional forces
D. Compressional forces

Correct Answer: Tensional forces

Explanation:

A Graben is a depressed block of the crust bordered by parallel faults, formed by tensional forces pulling the crust apart.

Incorrect! Try again.

42 Which of the following is an example of an antecedent river?

A. A river that flows underground
B. A river that changes course frequently
C. A river that maintains its course despite the uplift of land across its path
D. A river formed after the landscape was uplifted

Correct Answer: A river that maintains its course despite the uplift of land across its path

Explanation:

Antecedent rivers existed before the uplift of the structure they cross and eroded vertically fast enough to maintain their course (e.g., the Indus crossing the Himalayas).

Incorrect! Try again.

43 In the context of erosion surfaces, a 'Summit Accordance' often indicates:

A. Recent volcanic activity
B. Glacial deposition
C. A former extensive erosion surface (Peneplain) that has been dissected
D. Random erosion

Correct Answer: A former extensive erosion surface (Peneplain) that has been dissected

Explanation:

When mountain tops are at roughly the same elevation (accordant summits), it often suggests they are remnants of an uplifted ancient flat surface.

Incorrect! Try again.

44 What is the specific term for the boundary between the crust and the mantle?

A. Mohorovicic Discontinuity (Moho)
B. Gutenberg Discontinuity
C. Conrad Discontinuity
D. Lehmann Discontinuity

Correct Answer: Mohorovicic Discontinuity (Moho)

Explanation:

The Moho is the boundary between the Earth's crust and the mantle, defined by a distinct change in seismic wave velocity.

Incorrect! Try again.

45 Lapilli and Volcanic Bombs are examples of:

A. Intrusive rocks
B. Tephra / Pyroclasts
C. Lava flows
D. Chemical precipitates

Correct Answer: Tephra / Pyroclasts

Explanation:

These are airborne fragments ejected during an eruption. Lapilli are pebble-sized, while bombs are larger.

Incorrect! Try again.

46 The 'Ring of Fire' is located around which ocean?

A. Atlantic Ocean
B. Pacific Ocean
C. Arctic Ocean
D. Indian Ocean

Correct Answer: Pacific Ocean

Explanation:

The Ring of Fire is a horseshoe-shaped area around the Pacific Ocean basin where a large number of earthquakes and volcanic eruptions occur.

Incorrect! Try again.

47 Which type of volcano has gentle slopes and is formed by low-viscosity basaltic lava?

A. Shield Volcano
B. Lava Dome
C. Cinder Cone
D. Composite Cone

Correct Answer: Shield Volcano

Explanation:

Shield volcanoes (like Mauna Loa) are broad and gently sloping because runny basaltic lava travels long distances before cooling.

Incorrect! Try again.

48 In plate tectonics, what is a 'constructive margin'?

A. Where plates slide past each other
B. Where plates are destroyed
C. Where plates collide
D. Where plates move apart and new crust is formed

Correct Answer: Where plates move apart and new crust is formed

Explanation:

A constructive margin (divergent boundary) is where plates move apart, allowing magma to rise and create new crust.

Incorrect! Try again.

49 The term 'Denudation' includes:

A. Weathering, mass wasting, and erosion
B. Volcanism and earthquakes
C. Only erosion
D. Only weathering

Correct Answer: Weathering, mass wasting, and erosion

Explanation:

Denudation is the long-term sum of processes that cause the wearing away of the Earth's surface, including weathering, mass movement, and erosion.

Incorrect! Try again.

50 Exfoliation domes are primarily the result of which weathering process?

A. Carbonation
B. Frost action
C. Pressure release (Unloading)
D. Oxidation

Correct Answer: Pressure release (Unloading)

Explanation:

Exfoliation domes form when overlying rock is removed by erosion, reducing pressure and allowing the rock to expand and crack in sheets.

Incorrect! Try again.
