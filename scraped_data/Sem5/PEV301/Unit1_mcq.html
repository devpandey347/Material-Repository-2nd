1 Select the correct verb to complete the sentence: The committee _____ decided to postpone the meeting until next week.

A. are
B. have
C. has
D. were

Correct Answer: has

Explanation:

Collective nouns like 'committee' take a singular verb when the group acts as a single unit.

Incorrect! Try again.

2 Identify the error in the sentence: 'Neither the CEO nor the managers was aware of the software glitch.'

A. nor
B. Neither
C. managers
D. was

Correct Answer: was

Explanation:

When using 'neither/nor', the verb agrees with the subject closest to it. 'Managers' is plural, so the verb should be 'were'.

Incorrect! Try again.

3 Choose the correct sentence.

A. One of the engineers are resigning.
B. One of the engineers is resigning.
C. One of the engineer is resigning.
D. One of the engineer are resigning.

Correct Answer: One of the engineers is resigning.

Explanation:

'One of the [plural noun]' always takes a singular verb.

Incorrect! Try again.

4 Fill in the blank: Bread and butter _____ his only food.

A. were
B. is
C. are
D. have been

Correct Answer: is

Explanation:

When two nouns joined by 'and' represent a single idea or unit (like bread and butter), the verb is singular.

Incorrect! Try again.

5 Identify the correct verb form: The cost of all these articles _____ risen.

A. have been
B. has
C. have
D. are

Correct Answer: has

Explanation:

The subject is 'cost' (singular), not 'articles'. Therefore, the verb must be singular.

Incorrect! Try again.

6 Select the correct option: Ten miles _____ a long distance to walk.

A. have been
B. is
C. are
D. were

Distances, periods of time, and sums of money usually take a singular verb when considered as a unit.

Incorrect! Try again.

17 Select the correct sentence regarding tenses.

A. If I were a bird, I would fly.
B. If I were a bird, I will fly.
C. If I am a bird, I would fly.
D. If I was a bird, I would fly.

Correct Answer: If I were a bird, I would fly.

Explanation:

Hypothetical or contrary-to-fact conditions in the present use the subjunctive 'were' for all subjects.

Correct Answer: had left

A. since 2010

Explanation:

26 Modifier Error: Select the correct sentence.

A. Walking down the street, the trees looked beautiful.
B. The trees looked beautiful walking down the street.
C. While walking down the street, the trees were beautiful.
D. Walking down the street, I saw beautiful trees.

Correct Answer: Walking down the street, I saw beautiful trees.

Explanation:

In the other options, the modifier 'Walking down the street' incorrectly modifies 'the trees' (Dangling Modifier).

Incorrect! Try again.

31 Identify the sentence with a dangling modifier.

D. swimming

Explanation:

The list uses gerunds (swimming, hiking). The third item should also be a gerund ('riding'), not an infinitive ('to ride').

Explanation:

38 Identify the non-parallel structure: 'The manager asked him to write the report and that he should file it.'

C. file it
D. write the report

Correct Answer: that he should

Explanation:

It should be 'to write the report and to file it' (or just 'file it'). Mixing an infinitive phrase with a 'that' clause disrupts parallelism.
