1 What is the primary goal of DevOps?

A. To separate Development and Operations into distinct silos
B. To replace all human operators with automation scripts
C. To increase the complexity of software deployment
D. To shorten the systems development life cycle while delivering features, fixes, and updates frequently

Correct Answer: To shorten the systems development life cycle while delivering features, fixes, and updates frequently

Explanation:

DevOps aims to shorten the development lifecycle and provide continuous delivery with high software quality by bridging the gap between development and operations.

Incorrect! Try again.

2 Which acronym is commonly used to describe the core values of DevOps?

A. DRY (Don't Repeat Yourself)
B. ACID (Atomicity, Consistency, Isolation, Durability)
C. CAMS (Culture, Automation, Measurement, Sharing)
D. SOLID (Single responsibility, Open-closed, Liskov substitution, Interface segregation, Dependency inversion)

Correct Answer: CAMS (Culture, Automation, Measurement, Sharing)

Explanation:

CAMS stands for Culture, Automation, Measurement, and Sharing. It was coined by Damon Edwards and John Willis to define the pillars of DevOps.
