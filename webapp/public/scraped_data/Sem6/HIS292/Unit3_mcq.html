1 Which two brothers are credited with founding the Vijayanagar Empire in 1336 AD?

A. Harihara and Bukka
B. Rama Raya and Tirumala
C. Krishnadevaraya and Achyutadevaraya
D. Devaraya I and Devaraya II

Correct Answer: Harihara and Bukka

Explanation:

The Vijayanagar Empire was founded in 1336 by Harihara I and Bukka Raya I of the Sangama dynasty, reportedly with the blessings of Sage Vidyaranya.

Incorrect! Try again.

2 On the banks of which river was the city of Vijayanagar founded?

A. Godavari
B. Tungabhadra
C. Krishna
D. Kaveri

Correct Answer: Tungabhadra

Explanation:

The capital city, Vijayanagar (Hampi), was established on the southern bank of the Tungabhadra River.

Incorrect! Try again.

3 Which of the following was the first dynasty to rule the Vijayanagar Empire?

A. Tuluva Dynasty
B. Sangama Dynasty
C. Saluva Dynasty
D. Aravidu Dynasty

Correct Answer: Sangama Dynasty

Explanation:

The Sangama dynasty was the first dynasty, founded by Harihara and Bukka. It was followed by Saluva, Tuluva, and Aravidu dynasties.

Incorrect! Try again.

4 Who was the most famous ruler of the Tuluva dynasty, often referred to as 'Andhra Bhoja'?

A. Krishnadevaraya
B. Sadasiva Raya
C. Vira Narasimha
D. Devaraya II

Correct Answer: Krishnadevaraya

Explanation:

Krishnadevaraya (1509–1529) is considered the greatest ruler of the Vijayanagar Empire and belonged to the Tuluva dynasty. He was known as 'Andhra Bhoja' for his patronage of literature.

Incorrect! Try again.

5 The famous Battle of Talikota, which led to the decline of the Vijayanagar Empire, was fought in which year?

Explanation:

The Battle of Talikota (also known as the Battle of Rakshasa-Tangadi) was fought in 1565 between the Vijayanagar Empire and an alliance of the Deccan Sultanates.

A. Rama Raya
B. Devaraya II

9 Which Telugu literary work on statecraft was authored by Krishnadevaraya?

A. Amuktamalyada
B. Panduranga Mahatmyam

10 In the Vijayanagar administration, what was the Nayankara system?

A. A feudal system of military chiefs holding land
B. A system of village self-government
C. A judicial system for criminal cases
D. A system of revenue collection by priests

Correct Answer: A feudal system of military chiefs holding land

Explanation:

Under the Nayankara system, the king assigned land (Amaram) to military chiefs (Nayaks) in return for maintaining troops and paying a fixed annual tribute.

Incorrect! Try again.

11 The Ayagar system in the Vijayanagar empire was related to the administration of:

A. The Army
B. Foreign Trade
C. The Village
D. The Central Court

Correct Answer: The Village

Explanation:

The Ayagar system was a key feature of village administration where a body of 12 functionaries (Ayagars) managed village affairs.

Incorrect! Try again.

A. Dinar
B. Jital
C. Tanka
D. Varaha

Explanation:

The Varaha was the standard gold coin of Vijayanagar, featuring symbols like the boar (Varaha avatar of Vishnu).

The Stone Chariot is actually a shrine built in the shape of a chariot, dedicated to Garuda, the vehicle of Lord Vishnu.
