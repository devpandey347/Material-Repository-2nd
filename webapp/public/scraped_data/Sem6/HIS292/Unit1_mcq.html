1 Which chronicler wrote Taj-ul-Ma'asir, considered the first official history of the Delhi Sultanate?

A. Ziauddin Barani
B. Hasan Nizami
C. Minhaj-i-Siraj
D. Amir Khusrau

Correct Answer: Hasan Nizami

Explanation:

Taj-ul-Ma'asir was written by Hasan Nizami. It deals primarily with the events of Qutbuddin Aibak's reign and the early years of Iltutmish.

Incorrect! Try again.

2 Who authored the text Tabaqat-i-Nasiri, which is a vital source for the history of the early Turkish Sultanate up to 1260?

A. Shams-i-Siraj Afif
B. Minhaj-i-Siraj
C. Ibn Battuta
D. Al-Biruni

Correct Answer: Minhaj-i-Siraj

Explanation:

Minhaj-i-Siraj wrote Tabaqat-i-Nasiri and dedicated it to Sultan Nasiruddin Mahmud. It is the most detailed source for the early Ilbari (Mamluk) dynasty.

Incorrect! Try again.

3 Which famous traveler visited India during the reign of Muhammad bin Tughlaq and wrote the Kitab-ul-Rihla?

A. Ibn Battuta
B. Niccolò de' Conti
C. Marco Polo
D. Abdur Razzak

Correct Answer: Ibn Battuta

Explanation:

Ibn Battuta, a Moroccan traveler, visited India during Muhammad bin Tughlaq's reign and served as the Qazi of Delhi. His travelogue is known as Rihla.

4 Who among the following was known as the 'Parrot of India' (Tuti-e-Hind) and is a significant literary source for the Sultanate period?

A. Faizi

D. Mirza Ghalib
