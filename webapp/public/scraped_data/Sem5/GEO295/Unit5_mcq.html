1 Which term describes the scientific study of the origin, formation, classification, and distribution of soils?

A. Pedology
B. Hydrology
C. Petrology
D. Geomorphology

Correct Answer: Pedology

Explanation:

Pedology is the branch of soil science dealing with the formation, morphology, and classification of soils.

Incorrect! Try again.

2 Which of the following is considered an 'active' factor in soil formation?

A. Climate
B. Time
C. Relief
D. Parent material

Correct Answer: Climate

Explanation:

Climate and organisms are active factors because they provide the energy and agents (temperature, precipitation, biological activity) that act upon the passive factors (parent material, relief, time).

Incorrect! Try again.

3 The vertical section of soil extending from the surface to the parent material is known as:

A. Soil Structure
B. Soil Horizon
C. Soil Profile
D. Soil Texture

Correct Answer: Soil Profile

Explanation:

A soil profile is the vertical section through the soil showing the various layers or horizons.

Incorrect! Try again.

4 In a standard soil profile, which horizon is known as the 'Zone of Eluviation' or leaching?

A. A Horizon
B. O Horizon
C. E Horizon
D. B Horizon

Correct Answer: E Horizon

Explanation:

The E Horizon (Eluvial) is the layer where minerals and organic matter are leached out, often leaving it pale in color.

Incorrect! Try again.

5 Which soil horizon is primarily composed of organic matter such as decomposing leaves and humus?

A. B Horizon
B. A Horizon
C. O Horizon
D. C Horizon

Correct Answer: O Horizon

Explanation:

The O Horizon represents the organic layer at the surface, consisting of leaf litter and humus.

Incorrect! Try again.

6 The process of removal of silica from the soil and the accumulation of iron and aluminum oxides in tropical regions is called:

A. Gleying
B. Podzolization
C. Laterization
D. Calcification

Correct Answer: Laterization

Explanation:

Laterization occurs in hot, wet tropical climates where silica is leached away, leaving behind iron and aluminum oxides, forming laterite soils.

Incorrect! Try again.

7 Which soil type is characteristically found in the coniferous forest belts of the sub-arctic regions?

A. Podzol
B. Sierozem
C. Chernozem
D. Laterite

Correct Answer: Podzol

Explanation:

Podzols form under cold, moist climates and coniferous forests, characterized by an acidic, ash-grey leached horizon.

Incorrect! Try again.

8 Soils that are transported and deposited by agents like rivers, wind, or glaciers are known as:

A. Intrazonal soils
B. Zonal soils
C. Azonal soils
D. Residual soils

Correct Answer: Azonal soils

Explanation:

Azonal soils are immature soils without well-developed horizons, often because the material has been recently moved (e.g., Alluvial soils).

Incorrect! Try again.

9 What is the primary cause of soil salinization in arid and semi-arid regions?

A. Overgrazing
B. Excessive rainfall
C. Wind erosion
D. Poor drainage and excessive irrigation

Correct Answer: Poor drainage and excessive irrigation

Explanation:

In dry regions, excessive irrigation raises the water table; when water evaporates, it leaves dissolved salts behind on the surface.

Incorrect! Try again.

10 Chernozem soils, known for being rich in humus and highly fertile, are associated with which biome?

A. Tundra
B. Desert
C. Tropical Rainforest
D. Temperate Grasslands

Correct Answer: Temperate Grasslands

Explanation:

Chernozems (black earth) develop under temperate grasslands (steppes/prairies) and have a thick, dark A horizon rich in organic matter.

Incorrect! Try again.

11 Which type of soil erosion involves the uniform removal of a thin layer of soil from the land surface?

A. Rill erosion
B. Bank erosion
C. Sheet erosion
D. Gully erosion

Correct Answer: Sheet erosion

Explanation:

Sheet erosion is the detachment of soil particles by raindrop impact and their removal by water flowing overland as a sheet.

Incorrect! Try again.

12 When rill erosion is neglected and the channels become large and deep, it transforms into:

A. Wind erosion
B. Splash erosion
C. Gully erosion
D. Sheet erosion

Correct Answer: Gully erosion

Explanation:

Gully erosion occurs when rills deepen and widen into channels too large to be removed by normal tillage operations.

Incorrect! Try again.

13 Which conservation method involves ploughing across the slope rather than up and down?

A. Terracing
B. Mulching
C. Crop rotation
D. Contour ploughing

Correct Answer: Contour ploughing

Explanation:

Contour ploughing follows the natural contours of the land, creating water breaks that reduce soil erosion.

Incorrect! Try again.

14 What is the main purpose of planting shelterbelts?

A. To increase soil salinity
B. To store water underground
C. To reduce wind speed and prevent wind erosion
D. To produce timber for export

Correct Answer: To reduce wind speed and prevent wind erosion

Explanation:

Shelterbelts are rows of trees planted to block wind, thereby reducing wind erosion and protecting crops.

Incorrect! Try again.

15 Edaphic factors influencing the distribution of plants refer to:

A. Topographical features
B. Atmospheric pressure
C. Soil properties
D. Climatic conditions

Correct Answer: Soil properties

Explanation:

Edaphic factors relate to the soil, including its texture, structure, acidity, and nutrient content, which affect vegetation.

Incorrect! Try again.

16 Plants adapted to survive in dry conditions with very little water are called:

A. Xerophytes
B. Mesophytes
C. Halophytes
D. Hydrophytes

Correct Answer: Xerophytes

Explanation:

Xerophytes have adaptations like thick cuticles, spines, or deep roots to survive in arid environments.

Incorrect! Try again.

17 Which factor is primarily responsible for the 'Altitudinal Zonation' of vegetation in mountain regions?

A. Soil salinity
B. Decrease in temperature with height
C. Distance from the sea
D. Wind direction

Correct Answer: Decrease in temperature with height

Explanation:

As altitude increases, temperature decreases (lapse rate), creating distinct climatic bands that support different types of vegetation.

Incorrect! Try again.

18 The 'Wallace Line' separates the faunal regions of:

A. Asia and Australia
B. Europe and Africa
C. North America and South America
D. Antarctica and South America

Correct Answer: Asia and Australia

Explanation:

The Wallace Line is a faunal boundary line that separates the ecozones of Asia and Wallacea (transitional zone to Australia).

Incorrect! Try again.

19 Which of the following is a major anthropogenic cause of deforestation?

A. Forest fires caused by lightning
B. Tsunami
C. Expansion of agriculture
D. Volcanic eruptions

Correct Answer: Expansion of agriculture

Explanation:

The conversion of forest land to agricultural land (for crops or grazing) is the leading driver of deforestation globally.

Incorrect! Try again.

20 The concept of 'Social Forestry' primarily aims to:

A. Export rare timber
B. Manage forests for the benefit of local communities
C. Create private hunting grounds
D. Conduct genetic research on trees

Correct Answer: Manage forests for the benefit of local communities

Explanation:

Social forestry involves the management and protection of forests with the specific goal of helping environmental, social, and rural development.

21 Agroforestry is best defined as:

A. Growing only medicinal plants
B. Cutting down forests for agriculture
C. The practice of integrating trees with crops and/or livestock
D. Forestry on government land only

Correct Answer: The practice of integrating trees with crops and/or livestock

Explanation:

Agroforestry is a land-use management system in which trees or shrubs are grown around or among crops or pastureland.

Incorrect! Try again.

22 Which type of social forestry involves farmers growing trees on their own farmland for commercial or non-commercial purposes?

A. Farm Forestry
B. Extension Forestry
C. Community Forestry
D. Urban Forestry

Correct Answer: Farm Forestry

Explanation:

Farm forestry is the practice where farmers grow trees on their agricultural land for firewood, timber, or commercial sale.

23 What is 'In-situ' conservation?

A. Conserving species in botanical gardens
B. Conserving species in their natural habitats
C. Conserving species in gene banks
D. Conserving species in zoos

Correct Answer: Conserving species in their natural habitats

Explanation:

In-situ conservation involves protecting species within their natural surroundings, such as in National Parks or Wildlife Sanctuaries.

24 Which soil horizon is known as the 'Zone of Illuviation' or accumulation?

A. B Horizon
B. R Horizon
C. C Horizon
D. A Horizon

Explanation:

The B Horizon accumulates minerals (clay, iron, aluminum) leached down from the horizons above it.

25 The arrangement of soil particles into clumps or aggregates is referred to as:

Soil structure refers to how soil particles group together into aggregates or 'peds'.

26 Which biome is characterized by the highest biodiversity?

A. Tundra

C. Taiga
D. Savanna

Explanation:

Tropical rainforests have optimal conditions (warmth and moisture) allowing for the highest species richness.

27 Mangrove forests are typically found in:

A. Coastal saline or brackish waters
B. Deep desert oases
C. Freshwater lakes interiors

Mangroves are halophytes (salt-tolerant) found in intertidal coastal zones.

28 The breakdown of rocks in situ without transportation is called:

A. Weathering
B. Erosion
C. Deposition
D. Mass wasting

Correct Answer: Weathering

Explanation:

Weathering is the breakdown or dissolving of rocks and minerals on the surface of the Earth, serving as the first step in soil formation.

29 Which gas is primarily sequestered by forests, making deforestation a contributor to climate change?

A. Oxygen
B. Carbon Dioxide
C. Nitrogen
D. Argon

Explanation:

Trees absorb Carbon Dioxide; deforestation releases stored carbon and reduces the planet's capacity to absorb CO2.

30 The 'R' horizon in a soil profile represents:

Terracing converts a steep slope into a series of flat steps, reducing the speed of water runoff and soil erosion.

Ex-situ conservation involves protecting biological components outside their natural habitats, such as in seed banks, zoos, or botanical gardens.
