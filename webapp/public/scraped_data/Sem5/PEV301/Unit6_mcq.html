1 What is the primary purpose of the 'framing your viewpoint' stage in a Group Discussion?

A. To memorize a script
B. To contradict everyone else immediately
C. To establish a clear and logical stance on the topic
D. To wait until the end to speak

Correct Answer: To establish a clear and logical stance on the topic

Explanation:

Framing your viewpoint involves structuring your thoughts to present a coherent and logical argument that defines your position on the topic.

Incorrect! Try again.

2 Which of the following best describes 'Active Listening' in a GD?

A. Planning your next point while others speak
B. Fully concentrating, understanding, responding, and remembering what is said
C. Remaining silent throughout the discussion
D. Listening only to respond

Correct Answer: Fully concentrating, understanding, responding, and remembering what is said

Explanation:

Active listening requires full engagement with the speaker to understand their perspective, rather than just passively hearing words or planning a rebuttal.

Incorrect! Try again.

3 In the idea generation technique SPELT, what does the 'S' stand for?

A. Social
B. Scientific
C. Structural
D. Strategic

Correct Answer: Social

Explanation:

SPELT stands for Social, Political, Economic, Legal, and Technological factors, used to generate diverse points on a topic.

4 When using the SCAMPER technique for idea generation, what does the 'M' represent?

A. Monitor
B. Modify/Magnify
C. Manage
D. Make

In SCAMPER, 'M' stands for Modify (change an aspect) or Magnify (make something larger/stronger) to generate new ideas.
