1 What is the primary goal of 'Reading Comprehension' in a verbal ability context?

A. To memorize facts presented in a passage
B. To read as fast as possible regardless of understanding
C. To correct the grammar errors in a text
D. To understand, analyze, and interpret a written text

Correct Answer: To understand, analyze, and interpret a written text

Explanation:

Reading comprehension tests the ability to process text, understand its meaning, and integrate with what the reader already knows.

Incorrect! Try again.

2 Which reading strategy involves glancing rapidly through a text to search for specific information?

A. Extensive reading
B. Scanning
C. Intensive reading
D. Skimming

Correct Answer: Scanning

Explanation:

Scanning is a reading technique to be used when you want to find specific information quickly, such as a number or a name.

Incorrect! Try again.

3 What does 'Skimming' a text entail?

A. Reading every word carefully
B. Reading primarily for specific details
C. Reading aloud to improve pronunciation
D. Reading quickly to get the general idea or gist

Correct Answer: Reading quickly to get the general idea or gist

Explanation:

Skimming is used to quickly identify the main ideas of a text without getting bogged down in details.

Incorrect! Try again.

4 In the context of reading comprehension, what is an 'inference'?

A. A summary of the first paragraph
B. A statement clearly written in the text
C. A direct quote from the author
D. A conclusion reached on the basis of evidence and reasoning

Correct Answer: A conclusion reached on the basis of evidence and reasoning

Explanation:

Inferences are logical deductions made by reading 'between the lines' rather than information explicitly stated.

Incorrect! Try again.
